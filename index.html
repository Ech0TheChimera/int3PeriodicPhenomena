Periodic Phenomena

Driving Question

How can you identify functions in everyday phenomena?

What is a Periodic Phenomenon?

A Periodic Phenomenon is a phenomenon that reoccurs in regular intervals. It can often be described or modeled by a graph consisting of a dependant variable on the X axis and a dependant one on the Y axis. The X axis commonly models time passing, as it is most observable in day to day life, and the Y axis can model any number of things. In the test I ran, it models water drank in a given hour, measured in Liters.

Why Does it Matter?

Being able to reccognize and analyze data that has periodic patterns allows us to more easily model said data. Rather than going through and modeling every datapoint, you can fit a cosine or sine graph to it (If it is actually periodic). This allows you to, having recognized and modeled the trend, estrapolate future potential datapoints, assuming nothing changes.
